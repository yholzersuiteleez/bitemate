How it works

Three simple things. A whole planet of food.

01

Spin the globe

The hero of the app. A 3D globe of every country, with real recipes pinned by the people who live there. No algorithm picking for you — just a planet to explore.

02

Share a dish

Upload a photo. We'll figure out the country, the ingredients, and the dish name — you tell us what it's actually called in its home kitchen. It lands on the map.

03

Follow the feed

See what your people are cooking. Save what looks good. Score what you've made. Build a feed that tastes like the world, not the same five trends.

Why it matters

Food is the original universal language.

Every country has a way of saying welcome through dinner. The internet flattened a lot of things. It shouldn't flatten this.

Bitemate is built so that someone in Lagos, Seoul, or São Paulo feels equally at home — and so the recipe stays attached to the place it came from. No more orphaned dishes. No more "exotic" labels. Just food, with its passport intact.

The opportunity

Home cooking is the most-watched category online. Nobody has built a home for it.

Recipe content is fragmented across video apps, blogs, and screenshots in Notes. The connection between a dish and the place it came from gets lost. Bitemate organises food by where it actually lives — and gives a creator economy to home cooks, not just chefs with cameras.
